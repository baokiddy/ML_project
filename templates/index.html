Types of Clouds

Cirrocumulus

Cirrocumulus clouds are small rounded white/gray puffs that appear in long rows. A "mackerel sky" is when a lot of these clouds cover the sky - looking like the scales of a fish.

Cirrus

Cirrus clouds are made of ice crystals and look like long, thin, wispy white streamers. They are commonly known as "mare's tails" because they are shaped like the tail of a horse.

Cirrostratus

Cirrostratus clouds are high, thin sheet-like clouds that usually cover the entire sky. They are so thin that the sun/moon can sometimes shine through and appear to have a halo.

Cumulonimbus

Cumulonimbus clouds are thunderstorm clouds and are associated with heavy rain, snow, hail, lightning, and sometimes tornadoes. They can grow up to 10 km in height.

Altocumulus

Altocumulus clouds are mid-level, grayish-white with one part darker than the other. Altocumulus clouds usually form in groups.

Altostratus

Altostratus clouds are mid-level, gray or blue-gray clouds that usually covers the whole sky. The sun/moon may shine through these clouds, but will appear watery or fuzzy.

Stratus

Stratus clouds are low and have a uniform gray in color and can cover most or all of the sky. Stratus clouds can look like a fog that doesn't reach the ground.

Cumulus

Cumulus clouds have vertical growth. They are puffy white or light gray clouds that look like floating cotton balls.

Stratocumulus

Stratocumulus clouds are low, lumpy, and gray. Sometimes they line up in rows and other times they spread out. Only light rain falls from stratocumulus clouds.

Nimbostratus

Nimbostratus clouds form a thick, dark layer across the sky. They are often thick enough to blot out the sun.

Creating our model

Our approach to this project was to create and test different machine learning models that could classify clouds in images. Please study the diagram below to understand our process as we created and tested our model.


Choosing our Model

Our goal was to train and improve an image machine learning model. From our research, we found 5 feasible models:

  • AlexNet
  • InceptionResNetV2
  • DenseNet
  • ResNet
  • VGGNet

    • We decided to choose InceptionResNetV2 after evaluating it's accuracy after training it on uncurated image data.
